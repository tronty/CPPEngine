                                                    Matthew Stone loafbit 5 years ago edited

                                                    I recognize that picture epic gamer :)

                                                    But Markus' answer is why. When you calculate the normal with the cross product, the normals for the 3 triangle vertices are the same. Models can provide normals for the 3 vertices that are not the same -- making the surface shading appear more smooth. To see examples, look up smooth vs flat shading. The flat shading has the same normal for all 3 points (like the cross product gives). Smooth shading uses 3 interpolated normals.

                                                    To better understand a possible source of different normals, imagine a sphere made of triangles. Each point that makes up the sphere would have its normal calculated like "normal = normalize(vertex - center)". After the pipeline interpolates these normals (and you then normalize them, again in the frag shader), the lighting shading gives a more spherical shading effect.

                                                                                                                                      As some others already pointed out this tutorial has some errors in the explosion shader part. The explosion effect pushes the vertices in the wrong direction. Right hand rule of cross product also shows this. Either exchange vector a and b or compute the normal vector like this cross(b, a) or take other vertices for a and b.

                                                                                                                                      The second problem is that some faces are suddenly disappering during the animation. I am not 100% sure whats the exact problem, but I think the problem is that we cannot transform the position into clip space and then change the position without modifying position.w.

                                                                                                                                      As far as I know when transforming from view to clip space the projection matrix sets the w component to -z in perspective projections. So w depends on position.z. If we then change the position in the geometry shader without adjusting w there are some "inconsistencies" when performing perspective division. Perspective division is performed automatically after geometry shader stage. Vertices that should be inside the viewing frustum suddenly end up being outside because the resulting NDC is outside the [-1;1] range.

                                                                                                                                      This means the projection matrix has to be applied after the vertices are shifted along the normal in the geometry shader but not in the vertex shader.


                                                                                                                                      vec3 normal = GetNormal();
                                                                                                                                      gl_Position = projection * explode(gl_in[0].gl_Position, normal);

                                                                                                                                                                  pointsShader.use();
