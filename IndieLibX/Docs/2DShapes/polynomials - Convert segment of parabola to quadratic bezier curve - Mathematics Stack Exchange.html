Convert segment of parabola to quadratic bezier curve

Ask Question
4

How do I convert a segment of parabola to a cubic Bezier curve?

The parabola segment is given as a polynomial with two x values for the edges.

My target is to convert a quadratic piecewise polynomial to a Bezier path (a set of concatenated Bezier curves).

You can do this in two steps, first convert the parabola segment to a quadratic Bezier curve (with a single control point), then convert it to a cubic Bezier curve (with two control points).

Let f(x)=Ax2+Bx+Cf(x)=Ax2+Bx+C be the parabola and let x1x1 and x2x2 be the edges of the segment on which the parabola is defined.

Then P1=(x1,f(x1))P1=(x1,f(x1)) and P2=(x2,f(x2))P2=(x2,f(x2)) are the Bezier curve start and end points and C=(x1+x22,f(x1)+f′(x1)⋅x2−x12)C=(x1+x22,f(x1)+f′(x1)⋅x2−x12) is the control point for the quadratic Bezier curve.

Now you can convert this quadratic Bezier curve to a cubic Bezier curve by define the two control points as: C1=23C+13P1C1=23C+13P1 and C2=23C+13P2C2=23C+13P2.

Let f(x)=ax2+bx+cf(x)=ax2+bx+c be the parabola and let [x1,x2][x1,x2] be the interval of the segment that you want to convert to cubic Bezier curve. The first step is to convert that segment into a quadratic Bezier curve as follows:

1) The start point is P1=(x1,f(x1))P1=(x1,f(x1)) and the end point P2=(x2,f(x2))P2=(x2,f(x2)).
2) The control point CC should be the intersection point of the tangent lines at P1P1 and P2P2. These two tangent lines can be found as

y=(2ax1+b)(x−x1)+y1y=(2ax1+b)(x−x1)+y1, and
y=(2ax2+b)(x−x2)+y2y=(2ax2+b)(x−x2)+y2

Solving for the intersection point of above two line equations result in C=(Cx,Cy)C=(Cx,Cy) as

Cx=x1+x22Cx=x1+x22
Cy=x2−x12(2ax1+b)+f(x1)Cy=x2−x12(2ax1+b)+f(x1)

Once you have the quadratic Bezier curve, the cubic Bezier curve control points can be found as C1=13P1+23CC1=13P1+23C and C2=23C+13P2C2=23C+13P2
